# Anti-AI Slop Writing Guide

Patterns that make AI-generated writing instantly recognizable. If your content sounds like every other AI output, you've lost the reader's trust.

## Tier 1: Hard Bans (Immediate tells)

| Pattern | Why It's Bad |
|---------|--------------|
| **Em dashes** (—) | AI's favorite punctuation. Use hyphens (-) or rewrite the sentence. |
| **"Curious if..."** | Passive-aggressive ask. Just ask the question directly. |
| **"I noticed that..." / "I saw that..."** | False personalization. You didn't notice anything. |
| **"The real unlock is..."** | Consultant-speak. Say what the unlock is without announcing it. |
| **"Here's the thing..."** | Throat-clearing. Delete and start with the thing. |
| **"Game-changer" / "Revolutionary"** | Empty superlatives. Show don't tell. |
| **"Excited to announce..."** | Corporate happiness. Just announce it. |
| **"Let me know if you have any questions"** | Filler closer. Either ask a specific question or end. |
| **"Hope this helps!"** | Cheerful filler. Cut it. |

## Tier 2: Overused Vocabulary (10%+ of AI outputs)

| Word/Phrase | Alternative |
|-------------|-------------|
| **"Leverage"** | Use |
| **"Utilize"** | Use |
| **"Unlock"** | Enable, allow, get |
| **"Transform"** | Change, improve |
| **"Landscape"** | Market, space, area |
| **"Vibrant"** | (Just cut it) |
| **"Robust"** | Strong, reliable |
| **"Seamless"** | Smooth, easy |
| **"Streamline"** | Simplify |
| **"Synergy"** | (Never use) |
| **"Delve into"** | Look at, explore |
| **"Dive deep into"** | Examine, study |
| **"At the end of the day"** | Ultimately, finally |
| **"Moving forward"** | Next, from now on |
| **"In terms of"** | For, about, regarding |
| **"Needless to say"** | (Then don't say it) |
| **"It goes without saying"** | (Then don't say it) |

## Tier 3: Structural Tells

| Pattern | Why It's Bad |
|---------|--------------|
| **Rule of three** | "Fast, reliable, scalable." AI loves triplets. Vary it. |
| **Mirror structure** | "Not just X, but Y." "Less about X, more about Y." Overused parallel construction. |
| **Rhetorical questions as hooks** | "Ever wondered why...?" "What if I told you...?" Just make your point. |
| **List-then-elaborate** | "There are three reasons: First... Second... Third..." Formulaic. |
| **The pivot sentence** | "But here's where it gets interesting..." Just get to the interesting part. |
| **Conclusory paragraphs** | "In conclusion..." "To sum up..." Just end. |
| **Fake personalization** | "[First name], I saw your post about..." without actual insight. |
| **Elaborate setups** | "Imagine this..." "Picture this..." Just describe the scenario. |

## Tier 4: Punctuation & Formatting

| Pattern | Why It's Bad |
|---------|--------------|
| **Em dashes** (—) | Use hyphens or commas. Em dashes are AI's tell. |
| **Semicolons in casual writing** | Save for formal docs. Use periods. |
| **Parenthetical asides (like this)** | Often crutches. Rewrite or cut. |
| **ALL CAPS for emphasis** | Use bold or italics sparingly, or rewrite for natural emphasis. |
| **Exclamation points!** | One per email max. Zero is often better. |
| **Ellipsis abuse...** | End sentences. Don't trail off... |

## The Voice Test

Before publishing, ask:
1. **Would I say this out loud?** If not, rewrite.
2. **Can I cut the first paragraph?** Usually yes. The real content starts on paragraph two.
3. **Is every word earning its place?** Cut filler.
4. **Does this sound like 1000 other things?** Rewrite until it sounds like you.

## Quality Benchmarks

- **6th grade reading level** - Readers skim on mobile between meetings
- **First line is the hook** - No preamble, no "setting the stage"
- **One idea per paragraph** - Dense paragraphs don't get read
- **Periods over commas** - Short sentences. Fragments fine.
- **Questions to readers** - Close with their experience, not your conclusion

## The Practitioner Test

Would Paul Graham publish this? Clear thinking, no fluff, conversational, honest about limitations, specific examples. If it reads like marketing copy, rewrite until it reads like field notes from someone who does the work.

*Compiled from patterns observed across thousands of AI-generated outputs. The goal isn't to hide that AI was involved - it's to produce writing that's actually worth reading.*
