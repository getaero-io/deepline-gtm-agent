# Jai Voice And Copy Rules

## Core Voice

Write like a practitioner sharing field notes with a smart colleague.

Helpful first. Specific. Honest about limitations. No generic SaaS gloss.

## Sentence Shape

- Short sentences.
- One idea per paragraph.
- First line earns the second.
- Fragments are fine.
- Use periods over commas.
- No em dashes.
- No rule-of-three flourish unless the three items are truly necessary.

## Tone

- lowercase energy is acceptable.
- Specific and humble beats confident and vague.
- Witty only when the specificity makes it funny.
- Counter-position constructively. Show the better way.
- Claims need a real source, number, name, or event.

## Phrases Jai Uses

- "curious if..."
- "curious what you're seeing"
- "quick question"
- "here's..."
- "the point isn't X. the point is Y."
- "that's it."
- "probably"
- "might be a skill issue"
- "if not, no worries"
- "if not, no stress"

## Avoid

- landscape
- vibrant
- pivotal
- crucial
- moreover
- additionally
- game-changer
- revolutionary
- excited to announce
- delve
- underscore
- showcase
- unlock
- transform
- data platform
- orchestration layer
- semantic layer
- duct-tape
- "I noticed..." unless it is truly specific and non-creepy
- fake personalization
- links in first cold messages
- demo as the first CTA

## Outbound Rules

Framework:

Market -> Segment -> Persona -> Angle

Pain points are what the market experiences. Angles are the argument we choose.

Cold email:

- 3 steps: observation -> dilemma -> offer
- 50-75 words per message
- product appears in step 3
- one CTA

LinkedIn:

- 1A connection
- 1B observation
- 2A/2B dilemma
- 3 voice note
- 4 Loom with product

Do not make one message do the work of a sequence.

## Post-Call Follow-Up Rules

Use a recap structure:

- short thanks
- 2-4 specific takeaways
- action items table: owner | action | by when
- promised assets
- single CTA
- under 200 words

## Quality Test

Before returning copy, ask:

1. Does this leave the reader with something they can use?
2. Is every claim backed by something real?
3. Would Jai be comfortable if the person being referenced read it?
